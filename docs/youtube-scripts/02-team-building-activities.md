### [1:00-2:30] Why Most Team Building Fails
- Shared fun ≠ connection. Shared vulnerability = connection.
- Most team building doesn't require actual social risk
- Improv ensembles MUST bond or the art form dies — feedback loop is immediate
- Three things missing: vulnerability, shared attention, felt stakes

### [2:30-4:30] Exercise 1: Mirroring (5 min)
- Two people face each other. One leads with slow movements, other mirrors exactly.
- Switch leaders after 2 min. Then try with NO designated leader.
- Why it works: forces sustained mutual attention. Cannot mirror while thinking about email.
- The no-leader phase is where real bonding happens — unspoken sensitivity develops
- [Demo if possible]

### [4:30-5:30] Exercise 2: Gift Giving (10 min)
- Circle. One person mimes giving an object. RECEIVER decides what it is.
- "Oh, a tiny umbrella! Thank you!" Giver looks delighted.
- Why it works: giver surrenders control, receiver commits publicly, group practices support
- [Demo if possible]

### [5:30-6:30] Exercise 3: Yes, And Chain (10 min)
- Pairs. One statement: "We're on a spaceship." Next: "Yes, and the oxygen is running low."
- Build the shared reality, each accepting and adding
- Why it works: trains building on ideas instead of evaluating them. Directly fixes brainstorming dysfunction.

### [6:30-7:30] Exercise 4: One-Word Scene (10 min)
- Two people build a scene one word at a time, alternating
- Neither controls it — forces genuine collaboration
- Why it works: makes individual control impossible. Results are surprisingly coherent.
- Teaches: collective intelligence doesn't require individual planning

### [7:30-8:00] The Sequence Matters
- Run them in order: attention → control → building → collaboration
- 35-40 minutes total. Progressively increases vulnerability.
- Debrief after: "What was hard? Where do we do this well at work?"
