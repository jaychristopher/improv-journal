### [1:00-3:00] The Bandwidth Model
- Your brain has a limited processing budget — cognitive scientists call this bandwidth
- When you spend bandwidth on evaluating, simulating, rehearsing — there's nothing left for the actual moment
- Improv performers call this "internal computation" — the planning loop that steals attention from connection
- Key insight: overthinking isn't a willpower problem. You can't willpower your way out of it any more than you can willpower your way into having more RAM

### [3:00-5:00] What Improv Discovers
- Performers who monitor their own performance → worst shows
- Performers who forget themselves → best shows
- The paradox: trying to be good makes you worse
- Keith Johnstone: "The decision not to try and control the future allows students to be spontaneous"
- The first idea is almost always better than the third — the third exists because the first one scared you

### [5:00-7:00] Three Exercises You Can Try Today
**Exercise 1: Mirroring (2 min)**
- Stand facing someone. Mirror their movements exactly.
- Impossible to overthink while doing this — your attention gets consumed
- This is what presence feels like. No thinking required.

**Exercise 2: First Line Drill (solo)**
- Next time someone asks you a question, answer with the first thing that comes to mind
- Not the edited version. The first one.
- Notice: it's almost always better than what you would have planned

**Exercise 3: The Last Word Response**
- Make the first word of your response the last word of what they said
- Forces you to hear the END of their sentence instead of composing your reply
- Quality of your responses changes immediately
