## Instruction 14: Record + Upload Video #1 (How to Stop Overthinking) (60 min)

```
I'm recording my first YouTube video. Topic: "How to Stop Overthinking — The Bandwidth Problem"

SCRIPT OUTLINE (8-10 min video):

[0:00-0:30 HOOK]
"You know that thing where someone asks you a simple question and instead of answering, your brain launches a full simulation? 'What's the right thing to say? How will this land? What if I'm wrong?' By the time you've run the simulation, the moment is gone. You deliver something safe and dead. That's not a character flaw. It's a bandwidth problem. And improv performers have been solving it for 60 years."

[0:30-1:00 EARLY CTA]
"I wrote a full breakdown of this with exercises you can try today at physicsofconnection.com/how-to-stop-overthinking — link in the description. But let me walk you through the core insight first."

[1:00-3:00 BANDWIDTH MODEL]
- Brain has limited processing budget — bandwidth
- When you spend it on evaluating, simulating, rehearsing — nothing left for the actual moment
- Improv performers call this "internal computation"
- Key insight: overthinking isn't a willpower problem. You can't willpower your way out of having more RAM.

[3:00-5:00 WHAT IMPROV DISCOVERS]
- Performers who monitor themselves → worst shows
- Performers who forget themselves → best shows
- Trying to be good makes you worse
- Johnstone: "The decision not to try and control the future allows students to be spontaneous"
- The first idea is almost always better than the third

[5:00-7:00 THREE EXERCISES]
1. Mirroring (2 min) — face someone, mirror their movements. Impossible to overthink.
2. First Line Drill (solo) — answer questions with the first thing that comes to mind
3. Last Word Response — first word of your reply = last word of their sentence

[7:00-7:30 CLOSING CTA]
"These three exercises come from improv training, but they work in any conversation, meeting, or relationship. Full breakdown at physicsofconnection.com/how-to-stop-overthinking. Also worth checking out: Cal Newport's Deep Work for the focus angle. Links in the description."

[7:30-8:00 CLOSE]
"Overthinking isn't a personality trait. It's a bandwidth allocation problem. The fix isn't 'stop thinking' — it's redirect your attention to the actual moment in front of you. Try one of those exercises today."
```
