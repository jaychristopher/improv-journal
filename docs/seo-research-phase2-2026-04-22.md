## Key Insight: The Biggest Opportunity Is NOT Improv Keywords

Our highest-volume keywords aren't improv-specific — they're **life-skill queries** where improv provides a unique, differentiated angle that generic self-help sites can't match. BetterUp (a coaching platform) gets 1,000+ visits/mo from "types of listening" alone. We have deeper content on this topic than they do.

The unseen opportunity: **improv-informed answers to universal human questions.**

## Competitor Insight: BetterUp as Template

BetterUp ranks for our target keywords with generic coaching content. Their pages that overlap with our topics:

**Their strength:** Domain authority (DR ~80 vs our ~0). We need to win on content quality + specific long-tail keywords where DR matters less.

## Strategic Observations

### 1. We're sitting on a unique positioning
No other site combines improv expertise with life-skill content at this depth. Psychology sites give advice without exercises. Improv sites give exercises without life application. We do both. Every bridge should make this explicit.

### 2. The "improv" keyword isn't our biggest growth vector
Our highest-potential keywords don't contain the word "improv" at all. "How to stop caring what people think" (2,400/mo) has nothing to do with improv in the searcher's mind — but improv has the best answer. This is our moat.

### 3. BetterUp is the competitor to study, not improvencyclopedia
BetterUp ranks for our life-skill keywords with generic coaching content. They have DR but no depth. We can win specific long-tail keywords where content quality > domain authority.

### 4. The atom library is undermonetized
155 atoms with rich, sourced content — many map to high-volume keywords but have no bridge connecting them to search traffic. The atom-to-bridge pipeline is the most efficient way to create new content: the knowledge already exists, we just need the SEO wrapper.
