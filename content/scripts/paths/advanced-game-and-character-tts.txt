[teaching] The game beneath the game. Advanced pattern mechanics. Understanding that games aren't one thing. They're a taxonomy. Behavioral games, emotional games, verbal games, physical games, status games, mapping games, scenic games. Each type works differently.

[leaning in] I've never heard anyone break it down like that.

[warm] Most training doesn't. You learn "find the unusual thing" and "heighten it." And that takes you really far. But at the mastery level, you need to understand why a status game needs different fuel than a verbal game. Why a mapping game evolves differently than a behavioral one.

[curious] And you said games evolve? They don't just get bigger?

[teaching] That's the key insight. Beginners think heightening means louder, bigger, more. But games also pivot. They invert. They combine with other games. They transcend into something the pattern alone couldn't reveal. [short pause] The mastery-level improviser doesn't just ride the pattern. They sense when the pattern needs to transform.

[serious] And sometimes the most powerful move is breaking the pattern entirely.

[emphatic] But only when earned. Only when the break reveals something the pattern couldn't. Breaking a game for no reason is just chaos. Breaking it at the right moment is art.

[conversational] Okay, that's the game thread. What about character?

[warm] Character as discovery. And this is going to challenge a lot of what most improvisers have been taught.

[curious] How so?

[teaching] Most improv characters are built from the neck up. A funny voice. An attitude. Maybe a name and a job. And that gets you a sketch character. Fine for three minutes, flat at ten.

[matter-of-fact] So how do you build a real character?

[teaching] You don't build it. You discover it. Starting from the body, using Spolin's physicality, Bogart's Viewpoints. From status dynamics, using Johnstone's seesaw. From game, because UCB figured out that the behavioral pattern IS the character. From type-subversion, where you establish an expectation and then reveal depth underneath.

[leaning in] So the character surprises even the performer?

[emphatic] That's the goal. At the mastery level, you're not deciding who this person is. You're letting the body discover through movement. Letting status discover through transaction. Letting game discover through pattern. Letting emotion discover through honest response.

[curious] This draws from a lot of different traditions.

[warm] Spolin, Johnstone, UCB, Bogart. Each one cracked a different piece of the character puzzle. This thread brings them together.
