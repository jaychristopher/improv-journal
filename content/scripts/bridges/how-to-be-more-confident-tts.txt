[emphatic] There is, and it's cognitive. [serious] It shows up as hesitation, hedging language, broken eye contact. The subtle tension of someone monitoring their own performance while trying to perform.

[teaching] But when you commit fully to a choice, any choice, the evaluation loop stops. Your bandwidth is no longer spent on "was that right?" and becomes available for "what's next?" That freed bandwidth goes directly into engagement with the present moment.

[contemplative] And engagement is what confidence looks like from the outside. [pause]

[warm] Exactly. Confidence isn't generated by picking the right option. It's generated by closing the decision loop. Full commitment closes it. Half-commitment keeps it running indefinitely.

[curious] Bandura's research on self-efficacy connects to this, right?

[teaching] Directly. Bandura found that confidence is built primarily through mastery experiences. Not through choosing correctly, but through acting and seeing that the action produced a result. [serious] Directly. Fixed mindset treats failure as evidence of permanent inadequacy, so you avoid situations where failure is possible. Growth mindset treats failure as information, so you engage more freely.
