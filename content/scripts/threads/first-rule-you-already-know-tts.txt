[matter-of-fact] Totally. You accepted the cliff. It's real, it's high, it's dangerous. You kept your agency. You responded authentically to what's there. That's the move. Yes-and isn't compliance. It's acknowledgment plus response.

[curious] I want to zoom back out. You said every interaction is a chain of offers. What counts as an offer?

[teaching] Anything that advances the shared reality. A word. A gesture. A shift in tone. A silence — silence can be an enormous offer. Somebody gets quiet in a meeting, that's data. That's them telling you something about the state of the room. You can accept it or you can plow through it.

[curious] And most of us plow through it.

[matter-of-fact] Most of us plow through it. [short pause] Here's the thing I want to land on, because I think it's the skill that actually matters. [curious] And that's not it?

[emphatic] That's not it. The skill is *receiving* the offers that are already being made. And building on them. Instead of replacing them with your own. [pause] Because here's what's actually happening in a dead scene, or a dead meeting, or a dead conversation. It's not that nobody is making offers. Offers are flying around the room constantly. [contemplative] Everybody's in a different scene.

[warm] Everybody's in a different scene. And they're wondering why the scene is dead. The scene is dead because nobody's in it with anybody else.
