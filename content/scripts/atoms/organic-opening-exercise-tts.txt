[curious] Okay, so what about drilling specific types? Because a pattern game and an invocation are totally different muscles.

[teaching] Completely different. Pattern game is logical. It's "if this, then what." You're building a machine together — someone establishes a pattern, someone follows it, someone heightens it, and then someone has to feel when it's time to pivot to something new. The whole thing is about extrapolation and timing.

[matter-of-fact] Invocation is the opposite end. It's poetic. The ensemble speaks as one voice. You're not building a logical structure, you're building a feeling. Tonal modulation, collective rhythm. It's the closest improv gets to choral music.

[curious] And scene painting?

[warm] Scene painting is sensory. You're layering a world into existence. Each person adds a detail, and the discipline is — does your addition deepen what's there, or does it clutter it? There's a huge difference between "and in the corner, a clock that hasn't worked since 1987" and "also there's a dog."

[contemplative] One builds the world. The other just fills space.

[teaching] And that distinction — deepen versus clutter — that's a life skill. Think about any meeting you've ever been in. Someone raises a point, and the next person either deepens it or clutters it. Scene painting teaches you the difference in your bones.
