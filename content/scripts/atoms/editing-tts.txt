[curious] Okay, so let's get specific. What are the actual mechanics? Because I feel like people throw around "sweep edit" and "tag-out" without really distinguishing them.

[teaching] Sure. The sweep is the cleanest one. Someone from the backline walks across the front of the stage — physically wipes the scene away. Everyone clears. New scene. It's unambiguous, which is why it's the default in Harold. There's no confusion about what just happened.

[curious] And a tag-out is different because someone stays.

[teaching] Exactly. You tap one performer out, replace them, but the other person remains. Now you're exploring a different facet of that character or that pattern. And when you do a few of these in a row — that's a run. You're stress-testing a game across multiple contexts. Like, if someone's character has this specific way of giving advice, you tag in as their barista, their therapist, their mother, and each context reveals something new about the pattern.

[curious] Those runs are some of my favorite things to watch. When a team locks in on one and just goes.

[teaching] Because they're pure ensemble mind. Everyone on the backline is thinking "what's the next context that would be surprising but inevitable?" That's shared authorship happening in real time.
