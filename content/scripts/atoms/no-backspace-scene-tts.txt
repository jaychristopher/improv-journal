[curious] No Backspace Scene. What's happening here?

[teaching] Two players do a scene, three to five minutes. A coach watches for any attempt to retcon, undo, or contradict previously established facts. [short pause] When a retcon happens, "Actually, I meant..." or "Wait, no, we're not in a hospital," or even subtly ignoring an established detail, the coach calls "Keep it!" The scene does not rewind. The player must justify and continue from the unwanted thing, making it true.

[conversational] So the retcon attempt itself becomes real?

[emphatic] Exactly. You tried to undo something, the coach caught you, and now the fact that you said it is another established fact that has to be honored. Everything moves forward. Nothing gets erased.

[curious] What does this train?

[matter-of-fact] Irreversibility. The felt experience of path dependence. You are always living in the consequences of the last three seconds. [short pause] But the deeper skill isn't memory. It's justification. The ability to build forward from any point, including the point you didn't want to be at.

[contemplative] I imagine people freeze when they get caught.

[teaching] That's the most common failure. Players stop dead, afraid to move. The coach says "The scene didn't stop. Keep going." [short pause] The second failure is subtle retcons. Not saying "no" outright, but simply ignoring a detail until it decays. Harder to catch, more damaging. The third is over-monitoring, where players become so afraid of retconning that they stop making offers entirely. The coach has to say "Being bold is more important than being careful."

[curious] What drives the retcon impulse?

[serious] Almost always fear. Fear that the last offer was wrong. Fear that the scene is going somewhere you didn't want. [short pause] And here's what's interesting. Every time the coach forces you forward, the scene finds territory more interesting than what you were trying to get back to. Every time.

[warm] That's the deeper lesson. Irreversibility is not a punishment. It's the source of all dramatic stakes. If you could undo anything, nothing would matter. The permanence of your choices is what gives them weight.

[emphatic] The transfer is clean. In every scene, you're always building forward from where you are, not backward to where you wish you were. This exercise makes that habit physical.
