How a game transforms within a scene — not just repeating at higher intensity, but changing in kind. Four modes of evolution.

**Pivot** — The game shifts to a new but related expression. Hines documents this: "We started off playing 'we love the super bowl' and then it became 'Doritos are everything.'" The game didn't heighten along the same axis — it pivoted to a new expression of the same underlying absurdity (obsessive consumer enthusiasm). The pivot keeps the scene alive when the original expression has been explored. Risk: pivoting too early reads as abandoning the game; too late means the scene died waiting.

**Inversion** — The pattern reverses. After the apologizer has apologized for everything through escalating absurdity, the other character finally says "Stop apologizing" and the apologizer apologizes for apologizing. The mechanism of the game has become the subject of the game. Status inversions are a specific technique: a high-status character losing power, or a low-status character seizing it. The game evolves through reversal rather than escalation.

**Combination** — Two games merge. Primarily a Harold third-beat move: the game from one scene is applied to the world of another. Within a single scene, combination occurs when two characters each have their own game and the games start interacting. The combination produces a new dynamic neither game had alone.

**Transcendence** — The game becomes something larger than its mechanical pattern. The heightening atom calls this "the blowout" or "the button." The behavioral pattern reveals a genuine emotional truth underneath — the apologizer isn't just exhibiting a tic, they're revealing a deep fear of being unworthy. The game has transcended its mechanical origin and become about something real. This is where comedy meets drama.

**The two axes:** Hines describes vertical heightening (same game, bigger) vs. horizontal exploration (same game, new context). Game evolution adds a third dimension: the game itself changes. Vertical and horizontal moves keep the game stable; pivot, inversion, combination, and transcendence transform it.

You don't plan a pivot; you follow the scene honestly and the game transforms because the characters demand it. TJ & Dave scenes "heighten" by getting quieter, not louder — game evolution as a concept implies structural awareness their tradition explicitly avoids.
